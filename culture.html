Celebrating the Vibrant Culture of the Seven Sisters

Explore the traditions, festivals, and heritage that make Northeast India a cultural treasure.

Major Festivals of the Seven Sisters

Bihu Festival (Assam)

Celebration of Assamese New Year with traditional dance, music, and feasting.

Chapchar Kut (Mizoram)

A spring festival after jungle clearing, filled with dance, music, and feasting.

Hornbill Festival (Nagaland)

Known as the Festival of Festivals, it brings together **all Naga tribes for a spectacular display of dance, music, and crafts.

Kharchi Festival (Tripura)

A week-long festival honoring 14 deities, celebrated with rituals, sacrifices, and grand fairs.

Lai Haraoba (Manipur)

A ritualistic festival celebrating the creation of the universe, featuring traditional dance and storytelling.

Losar Festival (Meghalaya)

The Tibetan New Year celebration, marked by prayers, feasting, and traditional performances.

Shad Suk Mynsiem (Meghalaya)

A Khasi thanksgiving festival, celebrated with graceful dance performances and traditional attire.

Wangala Festival (Meghalaya)

The 100 Drums Festival, a harvest celebration honoring the **Sun God** with music and dance.
